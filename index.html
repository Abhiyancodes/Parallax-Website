UNESCO SITES OF NEPAL
KATHMANDU DURBAR SQUARE

Also known as Hanuman Dhoka, this former royal palace complex sits at the heart of old Kathmandu. Built up over centuries by the Malla and later Shah kings, it is still home to the Kumari, Nepal's living goddess, and showcases some of the finest Newar wood and stone carving in the valley. Much of the square was damaged in the 2015 earthquake and has since been carefully restored.

PATAN DURBAR SQUARE

Located in Lalitpur just across the Bagmati River, Patan Durbar Square was the seat of the Malla kings of Patan. Its courtyards are lined with ornate temples and palace buildings, including the striking Krishna Mandir, a stone temple in the style of a South Indian shikhara. The former royal palace now houses the Patan Museum, one of the finest collections of religious art in South Asia.

BHAKTAPUR DURBAR SQUARE

Bhaktapur, or Khwopa, holds the largest and best-preserved of the three durbar squares. The 55-Window Palace and the five-tiered Nyatapola Temple stand as highlights of Malla-era architecture, while the surrounding pottery and Taumadhi squares still hum with daily Newar life, craft, and festival.

SWAYAMBHUNATH

Perched on a hilltop above Kathmandu, Swayambhunath is one of the oldest religious sites in the valley and is popularly known as the Monkey Temple. Its dome is watched over by the painted eyes of the Buddha on all four sides, and the climb up its stone stairway rewards visitors with sweeping views across the city.

BOUDHANATH

Boudhanath is among the largest stupas in the world and the spiritual center of Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal. Its mandala-shaped structure represents the path to enlightenment, and the surrounding lanes are lined with monasteries, prayer wheels, and the daily circumambulations of pilgrims and monks.

PASHUPATINATH TEMPLE

Set on the banks of the Bagmati River, Pashupatinath is one of the most important Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. Its pagoda-style architecture and riverside cremation ghats make it both an architectural landmark and a living center of Hindu ritual, drawing devotees from across Nepal and India.

CHANGU NARAYAN

Believed to be the oldest surviving temple in the Kathmandu Valley, Changu Narayan sits on a quiet, forested hilltop east of Bhaktapur. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, its courtyard preserves stone inscriptions and carvings dating back to the Licchavi era, offering a rare window into Nepal's early history.

LUMBINI

Lumbini, in Nepal's southern plains, is revered as the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha. The sacred garden is centered on the Maya Devi Temple and an ancient pillar erected by Emperor Ashoka, surrounded by monasteries built by Buddhist communities from around the world, making it one of the faith's most important pilgrimage sites.

CHITWAN NATIONAL PARK

Nepal's first national park stretches across the lowland Terai and protects one of the last strongholds of the one-horned rhinoceros and the Bengal tiger. Its forests, grasslands, and rivers support hundreds of species of birds and mammals, and jungle safaris here remain one of the country's most popular wildlife experiences.

SAGARMATHA NATIONAL PARK

Home to Mount Everest and the Khumbu region, Sagarmatha National Park spans some of the most dramatic high-altitude terrain on Earth. Beyond the world's tallest peak, the park shelters snow leopards, red pandas, and the Sherpa communities and monasteries that have lived among these mountains for generations.
